# NNNN. Title (imperative, one line)

- **Status:** proposed | accepted | superseded by [#NNNN](./NNNN-...) | deprecated
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Authors:** Name, Name

## Context

What is the issue we're seeing that motivates this decision?
Describe the forces at play — technical, organisational, social,
political — without yet committing to a solution.

Cite specific code, benchmarks, dependencies, or external
constraints. The reader should understand *why* a decision is
even being made.

## Decision

What is the change that we're proposing or have agreed to?

State it as a directive: "We will…", "noyalib does X…",
"contributors must Y…". Avoid hedging; an ADR is a commitment, not
a proposal. (If it's still a proposal, the status is `proposed`
and the document describes the *option* under that name.)

## Consequences

What becomes easier or harder as a result?

- **Positive:** what does this enable
- **Negative:** what does this cost — performance, complexity,
  contributor onboarding, future flexibility
- **Neutral:** what changes in shape but is neither obviously good
  nor bad

Be honest about the downsides. An ADR that lists no negatives
is hiding something.

## Alternatives considered

What other options were on the table, and why were they rejected?

Each alternative gets a short sub-section with the same
"would have looked like X; rejected because Y" shape. The point is
to leave a paper trail so a future contributor can re-evaluate
without re-doing the analysis from scratch.

## References

Links to issues, PRs, prior art in other projects, design notes
in `doc/design/`, or external papers.
